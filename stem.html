Specialized Subjects in the STEM Strand

The Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) Strand is tailored for students inclined toward Math, Science, and Engineering studies. Below is a list of specialized and core subjects for STEM students:

List of Subjects

Subject Description
Pre-Calculus Solving real-life problems using analytic geometry, series and mathematical induction, and trigonometry.
Basic Calculus Covers limits, differentiation, integration, and solving real-life problems involving continuity, rates, and areas.
General Biology 1 Explores life processes at cellular and molecular levels, including energy transformation in organisms.
General Biology 2 Focuses on heredity, variation, and the diversity of living organisms, including their structure, function, and evolution.
General Physics 1 Mechanics, waves, heat, and thermodynamics using algebra, trigonometry, and calculus concepts.
General Physics 2 Electricity, magnetism, optics, and basics of atomic and nuclear phenomena.
General Chemistry 1 and 2 Covers matter composition, chemical transformations, kinetics, energetics, and basic Organic Chemistry concepts.
Work Immersion/Research Students address scientific or technological problems, conduct investigations, and present findings in a scientific forum.

List of Core Subjects

Subject Description
Earth Science Discusses the Earth's structure, processes, geologic history, and issues related to its resources.
Disaster Readiness and Risk Reduction Applies scientific knowledge to address man-made and natural disasters through practical solutions.
