What is Tea?

Tea was discovered by accident over 5000 years ago when leaves from a tea bush accidentally dropped into some boiling water and delicately flavored the liquid. Tea was originally drunk for its medicinal benefits, and it wasn't until the 1700s that it began to be consumed as the delicious beverage we know today.

Tea is prepared from the leaf buds and top leaves of a tropical evergreen bush called Camelia sinensis. It produces a healthy beverage containing approximately half the caffeine of coffee, while also aiding muscle relaxation and stimulating the central nervous system.

Making of Tea

  • Heat the pot before putting in the dry leaves.
  • Measure the dry tea exactly.
  • Use freshly boiled water.
  • Ensure the water is boiling when entering the pot.
  • Allow to brew for 3-4 minutes to obtain maximum strength.
  • Remove the tea leaves after the brewing period.
  • Ensure all equipment is clean.

Specialty Teas

  • Assam: Rich, full, and malty flavored tea.
  • Darjeeling: A delicate tea with a light grape flavor.
  • Earl Grey: A blend of Darjeeling and China tea, flavored with oil of Bergamot fruit.
  • Jasmine: Green tea dried with jasmine blossoms.
  • Lapsang Souchong: Smoky, pungent tea.
  • Sri Lanka: Pale golden tea with good flavor.
