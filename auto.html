Legal Requirements


The legal requirements for running a store include paperwork, licenses, business registration, and permits needed for franchise branches.

For example, business owners need to secure local and federal licenses, adhere to zoning laws, and ensure all signage complies with city regulations.

Store Manual


A store manual includes a set of guidelines that the establishment and its employees use to maintain standards. It covers everything from customer service to operational procedures.

This document ensures consistency and efficiency in the way the store operates, helping employees understand their responsibilities and the expectations of the management.

Day-to-Day Operations Procedures


Day-to-day operations refer to the routine tasks required to keep the store running smoothly. This includes managing stock, cleaning the store, and handling transactions at the cash register.

Employees must also handle customer inquiries, resolve issues, and perform safety checks on equipment and appliances used in the store.

Operation System


The operation system refers to the organized methods used to handle customer service, job time scheduling, cooking (for food-related stores), and general store operations.

It outlines how employees should interact with customers, ensure quality service, and carry out their tasks efficiently and on time.

Personnel


The personnel section of the store manual outlines how to recruit, interview, train, and evaluate employees. It also includes guidelines on salary structure and performance reviews.

Proper training and clear expectations help ensure that staff members perform their roles effectively and contribute to the store's overall success.
