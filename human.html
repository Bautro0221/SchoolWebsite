Introduction to Human Body


The human body is a complex structure that is organized into various systems that work together to maintain life and health. The skeletal system, for example, provides structure and support, while the muscular system enables movement. Understanding the body’s structure and function is essential for comprehending how we move, grow, and interact with our environment. This worksheet will focus on the major bones and movements of the human body, particularly the functions of the skeletal and muscular systems, and key anatomical terms used to describe movement.

Here are some important terms related to the body’s skeletal system:

  • Axial Skeleton: Includes the head, neck, and trunk.
  • Appendicular Skeleton: Includes the arms and legs.
  • Flexion and Extension: Movement types where the angle between bones decreases (flexion) or increases (extension).
  • Abduction and Adduction: Movements away from or toward the body’s midline.
