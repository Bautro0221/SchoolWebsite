Specialized Subjects in the Sports Track

The Sports Track is designed for Senior High School (SHS) students who are interested in pursuing careers in sports, including athlete development, fitness training, coaching, and more. Below is a list of specialized subjects in this track.

List of Subjects

Subject Description
Safety and First Aid This subject aims to equip learners with life skills and competencies in safety, injury prevention, and management in various sports and exercise settings. It also covers appropriate health and safety responses during emergencies. Course completion may lead to a certification from the Philippine Red Cross.
Human Movement This subject covers topics on the origin, control, and learning of motor skills appropriate for varied needs and skill levels.
Fundamentals of Coaching This subject covers the basic concepts and principles of coaching.
Sports Officiating and Activity Management This subject covers the concepts, principles, and mechanics of officiating, as well as the rules of specific sports. It also covers the management of fitness, sports, and recreation events. Learners will be certified by the National Sports Associations (NSAs) and TESDA.
Fitness, Sports, and Recreation Leadership This subject aims to equip learners with competencies for managing safe and effective programs in exercise, sports, and recreation.
Psychosocial Aspects of Sports and Exercise This subject helps learners understand individual and social factors that influence participation, performance, adherence, and psychological well-being in sports and exercise.
Fitness Testing and Exercise Programming This subject enables learners to administer tests and programs for physical fitness and performance enhancement.
Practicum (in-campus) This subject provides learners with hands-on experience within the school setting in their area of specialization, such as student athlete enhancement, practice coaching, practice officiating, and tournament management or fitness/sports and recreation leadership.
Apprenticeship (off-campus) This subject provides learners with hands-on off-campus experience in their area of specialization, such as student athlete enhancement, practice coaching, practice officiating, and tournament management or fitness/sports and recreation leadership. This course allows learners to explore opportunities for advanced certification.
