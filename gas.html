Specialized Subjects in the General Academic Strand (GAS)

The General Academic Strand (GAS) offers a broad selection of subjects from various tracks, making it ideal for students who have yet to decide on a specific specialization. Below are the specialized subjects for GAS:

List of Subjects

Subject Description
Humanities 1 These may be taken from any of the Humanities and Social Sciences subjects in the HUMSS Strand.
Humanities 2 Continuation of Humanities 1 with a focus on deeper cultural and artistic studies.
Social Science 1 Explores key concepts and topics in the Social Sciences, offering students a broad understanding of society.
Applied Economics Introduces students to economic principles and their applications in real-world scenarios.
Organization and Management Discusses the fundamentals of managing organizations and the roles of leadership and teamwork.
Disaster Readiness and Risk Reduction A subject from the STEM Strand focusing on preparedness and response to natural and human-made disasters.
Elective 1 Can be chosen from any of the Tracks or Strands based on student interest.
Elective 2 Another elective to further explore personal or academic interests.
Work Immersion/Research/Career Advocacy/Culminating Activity This may be a combination of the options provided, especially when there is a shortage of work immersion opportunities.
