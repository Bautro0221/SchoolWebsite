Aquaculture and Farm/Fishery Tools


There are a variety of hand tools designed for specific purposes in aquaculture and farming. These tools come in different types, shapes, sizes, and degrees of hardness. It is essential to use the right tool for the job to avoid damage, injury, or discomfort.

Hand tools can be classified into four categories:

  • Cutting, Pinching, Gripping Tools: Used for cutting, pinching, and gripping tasks. For example, pliers and cutters.
  • Striking Tools: Used for chiseling, punching, and riveting. Common examples include hammers and chisels.
  • Driving Tools: Designed to insert, tighten, loosen, and remove screws, bolts, nails, and other pointed items. Examples are screwdrivers and wrenches.
  • Struck or Hammered Tools: Used for forcing a bolt, pin, or rivet into or out of a hole. Examples include punches and chisels.

In addition to hand tools, different kinds of pliers, wrenches, and hammers are used for specific tasks in aquaculture and farming:

  • Pliers: Types of pliers include slip-joint, groove-joint, parrot nose, plumber's special, and needle-nose pliers.
  • Wrenches: Types of wrenches include combination wrenches, adjustable wrenches, socket wrenches, and flare-nut wrenches.
  • Hammers: Types of hammers include curved claw hammers, straight claw rip hammers, ball peen hammers, and hand drilling hammers.
