What is a Computer?

A computer is an electronic hardware device. It operates and functions under the control of instructions called programs stored in its memory.

It is a programmable electronic device designed to accept data, perform prescribed mathematical and logical operations at high speed, and display the results of these operations.

Evolution of Computers

The invention of the computer has been a long process. It took many years to develop the concepts and designs that we now use in the 21st century.

Key milestones in the evolution of computing include the Tally System by Early Cro-Magnon, the development of the first mechanical calculators like Leonardo Da Vinci's Mechanical Calculator, Napier's Bones, and Charles Babbage's Difference Engine. Many innovations contributed to the digital systems we use today.

Digital Dawn

Early computing was driven by the need for better numerical systems and the ability to store and process information. Innovations like the Pascaline, the Analytical Engine, and Boolean algebra formed the basis for later developments in computers.

Computers Go to War

During the Second World War, computers became essential for military purposes, including calculating artillery firing tables and cracking enemy codes. After the war, many technological advancements from wartime computing were adapted for civilian and commercial use.
