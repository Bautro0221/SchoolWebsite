Specialized Subjects in the Agricultural and Fisheries Arts (AFA) Strand

The Agricultural and Fisheries Arts (AFA) Strand prepares students with the knowledge and skills necessary for careers in agriculture and fisheries. These subjects cover various aspects of farming, animal production, aquaculture, and sustainable practices in agriculture and fisheries.

List of Subjects

Specialization Description
Agricultural Crops Production (NC I) Learn the basics of crop production, including soil preparation, planting, and cultivating crops in various agricultural environments.
Agricultural Crops Production (NC II) Build on basic crop production skills by learning advanced techniques in managing crop growth, pest control, and harvesting.
Agricultural Crops Production (NC III) Further develop expertise in crop production through specialized knowledge in crop management, market strategies, and optimizing production systems.
Animal Health Care Management (NC III) Learn about animal health management, including disease prevention, treatment of livestock, and maintaining animal welfare in farming environments.
Animal Production (Poultry-Chicken) (NC II) Focus on the basics of poultry farming, including breeding, feeding, and managing chicken for egg production or meat.
Aquaculture (NC II) Study fish farming techniques, including water quality management, breeding, and raising fish for commercial purposes.
Artificial Insemination (Large Ruminants) (NC II) Learn the artificial insemination process for improving livestock breeding and enhancing livestock quality and productivity.
Fish Capture (NC II) Understand the various methods of capturing fish in different aquatic environments, including net fishing, trawling, and other sustainable practices.
Fishing Gear Repair and Maintenance (NC II) Learn how to repair and maintain fishing equipment, ensuring that gear remains functional and safe for fishing activities.
Food Processing (NC II) Gain skills in preserving agricultural and fishery products through processing methods such as drying, canning, and fermentation.
Organic Agriculture (NC II) Focus on the principles of organic farming, including soil health, crop rotation, and sustainable practices that avoid synthetic chemicals.
Rice Machinery Operations (NC II) Learn the operation and maintenance of machinery used in rice farming, from planting to harvesting and post-harvest processing.
