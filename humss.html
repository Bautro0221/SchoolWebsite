Specialized Subjects in the HUMSS Strand

The Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) Strand is designed for Senior High School students who want to pursue fields related to social sciences and humanities at the university level. Below is a list of its specialized subjects:

List of Subjects

Subject Description
Creative Writing/ Malikhaing Pagsulat Develops practical and creative writing skills, introducing techniques for fiction, poetry, and drama writing. Students engage in workshops to refine their manuscripts.
Creative Nonfiction Introduces students to reading and writing Creative Nonfiction, enhancing skills in critical and creative thinking, with forms like autobiography and blogging.
Introduction to World Religions and Belief Systems Explores the tenets and practices of major world religions, fostering appreciation of similarities and differences to promote mutual understanding.
Community Engagement, Solidarity, and Citizenship Focuses on applying social science methods to community-action initiatives, emphasizing human rights, social justice, and participatory development.
Philippine Politics and Governance Introduces concepts of politics and governance, exploring the development of political institutions and structures in the Philippines.
Trends, Networks, and Critical Thinking in the 21st Century Helps students analyze global trends, develop critical thinking, and create scenarios addressing contemporary issues and interventions.
Disciplines and Ideas in the Social Sciences Explores basic concepts, influential thinkers, and methodologies in social science disciplines, with relevance to global and local contexts.
Disciplines and Ideas in the Applied Social Sciences Focuses on applied social sciences like Counseling, Social Work, and Communication, highlighting their applications in critical development areas.
HUMSS Culminating Activity/ Work Immersion Allows students to integrate their learning through creative culminating activities like exhibitions and performances as evidence of their learning.
