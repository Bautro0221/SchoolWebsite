Specialized Subjects in the Industrial Arts (IA) Strand

The Industrial Arts (IA) Strand focuses on equipping students with the practical skills and knowledge in fields such as automotive, construction, electronics, furniture making, and welding. Students will be trained in repair and maintenance services, installation, craftsmanship, and machine safety, with emphasis on industrial and engineering technologies.

List of Subjects

Specialization Description
Automotive Servicing (NC I) Learn the basic skills in automotive repair, focusing on the inspection, maintenance, and repair of vehicle engines, transmission, and other automotive systems.
Automotive Servicing (NC II) Advanced training in automotive repair, building on basic knowledge to include diagnostics, repair procedures, and servicing techniques for various vehicle systems.
Carpentry (NC II) Learn basic carpentry skills including the use of tools and equipment to build and repair wooden structures, furniture, and fixtures.
Carpentry (NC III) Advanced carpentry skills focusing on the construction of more complex structures such as framing, roofing, and detailed joinery.
Construction Painting (NC II) Learn the skills and techniques in painting and finishing, covering surface preparation, painting methods, and color application for construction projects.
Domestic Refrigeration and Air-conditioning (DOMRAC) Servicing (NC II) Gain expertise in the maintenance, repair, and servicing of domestic refrigeration and air-conditioning units, including diagnostics and troubleshooting.
Driving (NC II) Learn the fundamentals of driving, including road safety, basic vehicle operation, and traffic regulations.
Electrical Installation and Maintenance (NC II) Study electrical systems, wiring, and maintenance techniques for residential, commercial, and industrial installations.
Electric Power Distribution Line Construction (NC II) Learn to install and maintain electrical power distribution lines, including safety protocols, equipment handling, and construction procedures.
Electronic Products Assembly and Servicing (NC II) Gain skills in assembling and servicing electronic products, focusing on soldering, circuit assembly, and troubleshooting techniques.
Furniture Making (Finishing) (NC II) Learn techniques in furniture production, including finishing methods such as polishing, painting, and lacquering to achieve high-quality results.
Instrumentation and Control Servicing (NC II) Study the installation, maintenance, and servicing of instruments and controls used in industrial environments for regulating processes.
Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW) (NC II) Learn the process of welding using a wire feed technique for joining metal pieces, with applications in various industrial settings.
Gas Tungsten Arc Welding (GTAW) (NC II) Gain skills in using a non-consumable tungsten electrode to weld metals, focusing on precision and quality of joints.
Machining (NC I) Learn the basics of machining, focusing on manual machining techniques, such as turning, milling, and grinding for metalworking projects.
Machining (NC II) Advanced machining techniques including CNC operation, precision measurement, and quality control for complex parts and products.
Masonry (NC II) Study the basics of masonry, including bricklaying, block work, and stone setting for the construction of buildings and other structures.
Mechatronics Servicing (NC II) Gain skills in mechatronics, including the maintenance and repair of automated mechanical systems that integrate electrical and computer technologies.
Motorcycle/Small Engine Servicing (NC II) Learn to repair and maintain motorcycles and small engines, focusing on diagnostics, repair techniques, and engine performance optimization.
Plumbing (NC I) Learn the basic plumbing skills needed to install, maintain, and repair water supply and drainage systems in residential and commercial buildings.
Plumbing (NC II) Advanced plumbing skills, including complex installation and repair of plumbing systems, with an emphasis on safety and efficiency in larger systems.
Refrigeration and Air-Conditioning (Packaged Air-Conditioning Unit [PACU] / Commercial Refrigeration Equipment [CRE]) Servicing (NC III) Gain in-depth knowledge of servicing and maintaining packaged air-conditioning and commercial refrigeration units, with focus on high-level diagnostics.
Shielded Metal Arc Welding (NC I) Learn the basic techniques of welding using a coated electrode for joining metal, with emphasis on safety and proper execution of welding joints.
Shielded Metal Arc Welding (NC II) Advanced shielded metal arc welding skills, focusing on heavier and more complex materials and techniques for industrial welding applications.
Tile Setting (NC II) Learn how to install and finish tiles, including preparation, cutting, and laying tiles for floors, walls, and other surfaces.
Transmission Line Installation and Maintenance (NC II) Study the installation and maintenance of electrical transmission lines, including handling high-voltage equipment and safety procedures.
