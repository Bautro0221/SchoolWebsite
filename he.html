Specialized Subjects in the Home Economics (HE) Strand

The Home Economics (HE) Strand equips students with skills in various sectors such as garments, tourism, health, food and beverages, and community services. Students gain practical knowledge in providing essential services to others, whether in the family, community, or workplace.

List of Subjects

Specialization Description
Attractions and Theme Parks Operations with Ecotourism (NC II) Learn how to operate attractions and theme parks, integrating ecotourism principles to offer eco-friendly and sustainable tourist experiences.
Barbering (NC II) Gain expertise in providing haircuts, styling, and grooming services to clients, focusing on proper techniques and customer care.
Bartending (NC II) Learn bartending skills such as mixing drinks, serving alcohol, and customer service in a variety of hospitality settings.
Beauty/Nail Care (NC II) Master techniques in beauty and nail care, including manicures, pedicures, and applying cosmetic treatments to clients.
Bread and Pastry Production (NC II) Learn the art of baking bread and pastries, from dough preparation to baking and presentation, in a commercial setting.
Caregiving (NC II) Develop the skills necessary for caregiving, including providing personal care, hygiene, and emotional support to clients, particularly the elderly and children.
Commercial Cooking (NC III) Advanced cooking techniques for food preparation and presentation in commercial kitchens, including managing kitchen staff and operations.
Cookery (NC II) Learn the fundamentals of cooking, including food preparation, cooking methods, and presentation in various culinary settings.
Dressmaking (NC II) Gain skills in designing and making garments, from pattern creation to stitching and finishing techniques for fashion pieces.
Events Management Services (NC III) Study the logistics and organization of events, from planning and coordinating to executing successful functions and gatherings.
Fashion Design (Apparel) (NC III) Learn the principles of fashion design, including creating apparel collections, fabric selection, and garment construction techniques.
Food and Beverage Services (NC II) Learn how to manage food and beverage services, from taking orders to serving food and drinks in hospitality settings.
Front Office Services (NC II) Understand the role of front office services in hospitality, including customer service, reservation management, and handling guest relations.
Hairdressing (NC II) Learn basic hairdressing skills, including cutting, coloring, and styling hair in a professional salon setting.
Hairdressing (NC III) Build advanced hairdressing skills, including styling for special occasions, hair treatments, and providing exceptional customer service.
Handicraft (Basketry, Macrame) (Non-NC) Learn to create handicrafts such as baskets and macrame items using various materials and traditional crafting techniques.
Handicraft (Fashion Accessories, Paper Craft) (Non-NC) Explore creating fashion accessories and paper crafts, focusing on design, crafting, and customization.
Handicraft (Needlecraft) (Non-NC) Learn needlecraft techniques such as embroidery, stitching, and other fabric-based crafts for creative design work.
Handicraft (Woodcraft, Leathercraft) (Non-NC) Study woodworking and leatherworking skills, creating handmade items such as furniture, decorations, and accessories.
Housekeeping (NC II) Master the skills of housekeeping, including cleaning, organizing, and maintaining guest rooms and public spaces in hotels or private residences.
Local Guiding Services (NC II) Learn to provide guiding services for local tours, focusing on cultural, historical, and natural aspects of the area.
Tailoring (NC II) Gain skills in tailoring garments, including measuring, cutting, and fitting clothes for clients, with an emphasis on precision and finishing.
Tourism Promotion Services (NC II) Learn to promote tourism services and attractions, including marketing, customer relations, and event organization in the tourism sector.
Travel Services (NC II) Study the travel industry, including travel planning, booking, and customer service for clients seeking travel and vacation experiences.
Wellness Massage (NC II) Learn the fundamentals of wellness massage, including techniques for relaxation, muscle relief, and improving physical well-being.
