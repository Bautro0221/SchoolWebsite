Organization and Management


Definition and Functions of Management


Management is defined differently by various experts based on their experiences. Mary Parker Follett described it as the art of getting things done through people. Frederick W. Taylor emphasized achieving tasks in the most economical way, while Harold Koontz focused on getting things done by people in formally organized groups.

Managers are responsible for coordinating and overseeing the work of individuals and groups to ensure effectiveness (achieving desired results) and efficiency (producing maximum output with minimum input). As Peter Drucker stated, "Efficiency is doing things right; effectiveness is doing the right things."

The Five Functions of Management

  • Planning: Setting organizational goals and defining strategies to achieve them.
  • Organizing: Identifying tasks, allocating resources, and ensuring harmonious relationships.
  • Staffing: Hiring the right people for the right jobs based on organizational needs.
  • Leading: Motivating and guiding employees to achieve organizational goals.
  • Controlling: Monitoring progress and making adjustments to stay on track.
